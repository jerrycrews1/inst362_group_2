Interaction Design Requirements

Interaction Design Requirement - Work Activity Note

  1. The system shall provide resources for assistance available outside of the UMD Health Center. - Unwilling to use mental health assistance because having to go to UMD health center (I:P3) and Received assistance for mental health off-campus (I:P1)
  2. The system shall provide mental health assistance online - Rather seek assistance in an online setting (chat, video call) (I:P3)
  3. The system shall provide resources for assistance available in person - Would rather seek mental health assistance in person (more personal interaction) (I:P2) and Prefers face to face communication with therapist or other resource (I:P1)
  4. The system shall provide accurate timing of available resources in one place - Need to sort out their timing online because they have two different timings and they are very confusing (I:P5)
  5. The system shall provide resources that are available 24 hours a day - More helpful if 24 hour mental health assistance over phone exists (I:P3)
  6. The system shall provide anonymous assistance through the use of a chat feature - Anonymous chat to provide mental health assistance will attract freshmen (I:P3)
  7. The system shall provide resources to freshman via email - Advises to use email as a way to gain more eyes, especially freshmen (I:P1)
  8. The system shall provide resources to freshman to deal with being away from home- Stressed from being away from home (I:P3)
  9. The system shall provide resources to freshman to deal with the change from high school to college routine - Overload of school assignments (I:P3) and Stressful finding a rhythm at UMD (I:P2) and Adjustment to college life was difficult, felt unprepared (I:P1)
  10. The system shall provide constant support and resources to freshman throughout the semester - At the end of the year mental state is very low for reasons such as no friends to talk to and no family support (I:P4) and Mentally and physically exhausted as the semester went through (I:P3)

Design Requirements Reflection

We discovered numerous interesting and thought-provoking responses from our interviewers through our interviews and work activity notes, which directed us in thinking about many alternative possibilities for developing a design. One thing we found fascinating is that the interviewee provided so much good feedback on what the health center could improve because they believe there are many things the health center cannot provide for them. One of the fascinating things we discovered was that some interviewees preferred face-to-face consultations over internet consultations. This surprised us because we assumed that most people seeking mental health advice would prefer an online platform where they could consult from the comfort of their own home or a secure location. One of the most difficult aspects of this process, in my opinion, has been the duplication of some of the answers we received from the interviewers. Regardless of whether they are in their freshman or senior year, or if they have transferred to the school recently, the majority of the students appear to have a similar difficulty with the health facility. It restricts our ability to make significant adjustments to the design. I believe one of the issues we encountered as a group was determining the best way to compose our work activity notes. Work activity notes are critical to ensuring that we can distinguish between the many distinct categories that our interviewees respond to and that we can focus on the specific improvement that our interviewees desire from the health center. As a result, we had to double-check our work activity notes process to ensure they were completed appropriately.

Concept Statement

The UMD Mental Health Assistance System will replace the old behavioral health services provided by our client: the University of Maryland Health Center. It will provide 24-hour-a-day services to general students, specifically to support freshmen students suffering from mental health disorders. This service includes online mental health assistance for those who want to have an anonymous setting. The new system includes chatting and video calls for those who live away from the campus. Setting appointments with mental health assistants will also be available through our system for those who want to have personal interaction. Furthermore, to acknowledge students of our existence, we will send weekly emails to students to seek our services if they are suffering from mental health disorders. Overall, previous behavioral health services failed to provide a variety of options, resulting in extensive waiting time and avoidance in seeking treatment. Compared to behavioral health services, our system will provide a variety of options to encourage students to seek mental health assistance comfortably. We will implement a UX solution that guides and encourages our users, freshmen students, who experience mental health issues to seek our system.

Challenge Statement Reflection

After doing this exercise, our team felt that there was much more clarity regarding the key points of our concept statement. Our system concentrates on the aspects of mental health and the appropriate assistance that our interviews and observations pointed towards. This is especially true when it comes to face-to-face vs an anonymous setting, which we found to be very divided among students. Looking back at our previous statement, we did not have enough detail and elaboration as to what our system would specifically cater towards and why we feel such things are necessary. Stemming from the knowledge we now know, our concept statement is able to include a wide variety of students, specifically freshmen, and their own unique needs and their preferred methods of communication and guidance.
