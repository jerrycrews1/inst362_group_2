Evaluation

Evaluation Plan

To evaluate our design, our team decided to ask ten user testers. We decided to include this many people because our team's design will get a lot more feedback from people with different information-seeking behaviors. After interacting with our team's design features, they will be able to point out flaws and points where they had difficulty completing certain tasks. Our team decided to recruit UMD students; to find suitable user testers, our team went to a freshmen event in the Stamp Student Center and asked our friend to recruit freshmen and transfer students to test our team’s design. Since our design is to help freshmen students seek the UMD health center for mental health treatment, we mainly recruited freshmen and transfer students who never had prior university experiences. This evaluation aims to find overall student experiences while interacting with main design features, discover how long it takes for students to complete a task from the main menu, design flaws that confuse students, and innovative design ideas to improve our team's design. The task that our team will ask user testers to complete is navigating through our main design features. For example, our team will ask users to book appointments, download VR systems, chat through the Anonymous Chat feature, navigate through the Meet Outside feature, and call the 24-hour hotline.

Script

  1. Introduction

    Hello, my name is (team member’s name); thank you for agreeing to participate in the design evaluation.

    I’d like to start by asking you some questions about who you are and your current grade. I will then ask you to perform some tasks on our new UMD’s behavioral health services application that is designed to help students.

    Once the task is completed, I’d like to get some feedback on overall experiences with our application design. This is to get an overall evaluation of our application design from you, so your honest feedback is very important.

    Are there any questions before we start?

  2. Background questions and ice breaking questions

    So, (user tester), can you tell us a little bit about yourself.

    Since this is your first and second semester as a (freshmen or transfer student), how was your overall experience as a student in the UMD?

  3. Provide an introduction to the product

    Before we begin performing tasks, I want to tell you a little bit about our product. The UMD Mental Health Assistance System will replace the old behavioral health services provided by the University of Maryland Health Center.

    Since previous behavioral health services failed to provide a variety of options, resulting in extensive waiting time and avoidance in seeking treatment, our product design will provide 24-hour-a-day services to general students, especially to support freshmen students seeking mental health assistance.

    The new system includes anonymous chatting, appointment scheduler, opportunity to meet outside of the health center, and VR meetings for those who live away from the campus and can’t commute to get assistance.

  4. Tasks

    To remind you, we really need honest feedback from you to evaluate the overall design features of our product.

    When interacting with our application, try to act as natural as possible, just like how you would interact with any other mobile application.

    While interacting with our application, we really want to hear your thoughts, like general experience while you are navigating through each feature, why you touch something, or if features need instructions or it is self-explanatory.

    So let’s begin. Ask:

    • How to navigate through the app in general.
    • How would you try to book yourself an appointment?
    • How would you try to get on the anonymous chat with the health professional?
    • How would you navigate yourself to the Meet Outside feature?
    • How would you try to get on the 24-hotline and call?

    Follow up questions:

    • Did you have any difficulty with navigating through the application?
    • Was there anything you thought initially about the design itself other than how easy it looked to navigate?
    • Did you have any issues downloading VR software?
    • Was there anything about scheduling appointments that you had trouble with?
    • Did you have any issues figuring out how to use VR software?
    • Is the scheduling here any different, or do you have anything you wish was different?

  5. Conclusion

    Thank you very much for completing the tasks.

    Before we end this evaluation, did you like the overall layout of the application?

    Would you use this application if you experience mental health concerns while in school?

    Thank you very much for your honest feedback; it was very helpful. Do you have anything you’d like to add before we wrap up?

    Again, thank you very much for taking time off of your day to participate in this evaluation. Your inputs were extremely helpful. Have a great day!

Website Accessibility

Website Accessibility We were lucky enough to not have any errors when using the WAVE tool. Jerry has developed websites in the past and has used this tool each time so he already knew what to do when developing the website. There are drawbacks to utilizing a tool like WAVE instead of testing on users with disabilities. Tools like WAVE cannot catch every error every time. CSS plays a huge role in how a website looks and that is not always picked up by the WAVE tool. Javascript can also affect the accessibility of a website that a disabled user may notice while using the website but a tool like WAVE may not. The biggest benefit to using a tool like WAVE is that it helps reduce the time and cost of making our website accessible. It is not always possible to include disabled users and even if we do, we may not include all different disabilities that users may have. Using the WAVE tool can also be a great first step to making a website accessible. Ensuring the website doesn’t have any WAVE errors before implementing user testing with users with disabilities can help speed up the iteration process.
