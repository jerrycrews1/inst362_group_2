Contextual Analysis

Motivation

The motivation for the project is to better understand and potentially encourage first-year students who are struggling with mental health disorders to seek mental health advisors. Freshman year is a challenging year for many students since it is the first year they are away from home or on their own. It is also an adjustment period to the difficulties of balancing academic success in college with a social life. According to the annual Beginning College Survey of Student Engagement, they found that "53 percent of first-year students reported a substantial increase in mental and emotional exhaustion" (Carrasco, 2021). Although Universitiesâ€™ Health Center offers several links and phone numbers for freshmen to use for mental health services, the majority of students do not seek or receive treatment due to the perception that treatment is not needed, lack of time, preference for self-management, pragmatic barriers to accessing services (long waiting period, financial concerns, not knowing where to go), concerns about stigma and discomfort related to discussing problems with professionals, doubt that professional help would be beneficial, and negative experiences with professional help-seeking (Czyz et al., 2013). Our project will be to understand how to better provide mental health services to freshmen. Our client, UMD health officials, will better understand the restrictions freshmen experience when seeking mental health assistance through our findings. Furthermore, with the help of the interviews and observations, we will implement a UX solution that guides and encourages our users, freshmen students, who experience mental health issues to seek UMD health officials.

Flow Model

The first major work role is of the freshman/transfer students at UMD. Specifically, in our project, the work role of the students we are interested in are those seeking mental health assistance (or not seeking it because of some reason other than not needing it). These students are currently accomplishing their tasks several ways. Some do not seek help from the official work roles of counselors but instead turn to their friends for assistance (I:P2). While we did not have friends in our initial thought process, we have added it to the flow model because of our interviews.

The second work role is that of friend. The friend, in our diagram, provides support and assistance to the student work role. We may require followup interviews to better understand this work role.

The third work role is the UMD Counselor/Health Worker. This work role provides mental health assistance to the student in our diagram. This work role would not be able to help assist the student without the UMD Health Center and/or the UMD Counseling Center. The UMD Counselor/Health Worker role also uses multiple UMD websites to provide students with the information they need to seek mental health assistance.

We list Third-Party Health Workers very close to the UMD Counselors/Health Workers. We do this because these nodes are separate, but they perform similar functions. They both provide mental health assistance to the student, but the Third-Party Health Worker does not work at the UMD Health Center/Counseling Center. This work role is a resource that is provided on the UMD website.

The two entities that we have identified are the UMD Health Center/Counseling Center and multiple UMD websites. These entities facilitate the seeking and providing of mental health assistance between the student and the UMD Health Workers/Counselors.

Work Activity Affinity Diagram

Our contextual analysis helped us identify several key categories. The first is the general mood and mental health of the students during their freshman/first year at the university. We were able to identify that several of the students felt that the adjustment to college was stressful and difficult. Every one of the students felt stressed during their first semester.

The next category we identified was the reasons for stress that the students identified. There was a lot of variety in the reasons for stress. One of the students has a family and had a long commute to and from campus. Another student had never needed to study before. The theme that we identified here was that there are a lot of reasons why students are stressed during their first semester.

The third category that became apparent was how the interviewees dealt with their stress. Only one of our interviewees had received some sort of mental health counseling. This person received their counseling from an off-campus resource. Interestingly, one of the interviewees specifically stated that he liked to deal with his stress on his own. This student stated that he enjoyed the challenge. This was not something that we expected to find throughout our interviews.

The next theme we identified was how familiar the interviewees were with the resources available to them on campus. Two students stated that they did not know where to seek mental health assistance. Two others stated that they were familiar with the resources offered on campus.

The next theme was the interviewees willingness to seek mental health assistance. Two of the students stated that they would prefer face-to-face assistance if they were to seek it. Another stated that they would seek assistance if they knew it existed. We found a couple of the notes specifically interesting. One of the interviewees stated that they would not seek assistance because it was at the health center. Another stated that they would rather seek assistance in an online format.

The last theme we identified was the recommendations that resulted from our interviews. There were a wide variety of responses. One student stated they had no recommendations for how the university could help students. Some stated more sharing of the resources would be helpful. Furthermore, students had difficulties finding resources that they were looking for due to the complexity of the UMD health center website layout.

Reflection

One of the things we found interesting was the variety of responses we received when we asked the interviewees about their knowledge of resources available to them. Because most of our group members are seniors, we are very familiar with what resources are available. We were very surprised to hear from two of the interviewees that they were not familiar with the mental health resources that the university provides. This made us stop and take a step back. We thought that our problem was that students were stressed but did not want to receive mental health assistance from the university for whatever reason (our goal was to find this reason). Understanding that some of the students arenâ€™t familiar with the resources at all poses a different problem. Back to the variety of responses, some of our interviewees were very familiar with the resources available. They cited having received emails informing them of how they could seek mental health assistance.

Another thing that we discovered throughout the contextual analysis process that was surprising to us was the different reasons the interviewees stated they might not seek mental health assistance on campus. One student stated that he thought of solving his own mental health concerns as a personal challenge. This same student stated that he would likely seek professional assistance if he did not believe he could handle his concerts on his own. Another student stated that they would not seek assistance on campus because it was located at the health center. We had not considered the physical location of the health center to be a potential reason students were not seeking mental health assistance.

Reflections

Overall, our contextual inquiry went as we planned. We conducted interviews and observed UMD Health Center as we planned. The division of work among our team members allowed each member to focus on one task. Through our observation, we got to see how people behaved and utilized the health center. We were able to understand their needs and overall tasks. Through our interviews, we were able to obtain the mental state of students during freshman year and how they dealt with those issues. Although many more tasks are to be completed, our team feels like we have a big picture understanding of designing ideas for supporting UMD freshmen. If we had time to do more interviews and observations, our team would focus our interviews on freshmen students. It would be helpful for us to understand their current mental state and if freshmen students are aware of support for mental health disorders. For the observation site, we would observe where freshmen are commonly seen to observe the overall mood of freshmen students to see if they are stressed or anxious.
