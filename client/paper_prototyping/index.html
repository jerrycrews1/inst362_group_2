Concept Statement

The UMD Mental Health Assistance System will replace the old behavioral health services provided by our client: the University of Maryland Health Center. It will provide 24-hour-a-day services to general students, especially to support freshmen students seeking mental health assistance. This service includes online mental health assistance for those who want to have an anonymous setting, for those seeking other resources, and for those who would like to meet outside of the UMD Health Center. The new system includes anonymous chatting, appointment scheduler, opportunity to meet outside of the health center, and VR meetings for those who live away from the campus and can’t commute to get assistance. The new design will be more inclusive of disabled students who wish to seek mental health treatment; our design will include various features, for example, a video with clear audio and subtitles to discuss treatment methods and processes to provide clear guidance. Furthermore, to acknowledge students of our existence, we will send weekly emails to students to seek our services if they are suffering from mental health disorders. Overall, previous behavioral health services failed to provide a variety of options, resulting in extensive waiting time and avoidance in seeking treatment. Compared to behavioral health services, our system will provide a variety of options to encourage students to seek mental health assistance comfortably. We will implement a UX solution that guides and encourages our users, primarily freshmen students, who experience mental health issues to seek our system.

Tasks and Features of our Prototype

Anonymous Chat

One of the main features that we have is an anonymous chat function. This feature is essential because many students avoid mental health treatment due to fear of stigma. The stigma around mental illness is a significant issue in our society; seeking professional help for mental illness is seen as weak and often followed by discrimination, a substantial barricade for people to seek mental health treatment (Borenstein, 2020). Thus, our application provides an anonymous chat function to prevent this issue. Without having to set up appointments, students can easily find and talk to professionals about their current mental illnesses. Furthermore, our anonymous chat feature can be used by any student without having to log into their account; it will protect students' confidentiality and ensure problem sharing is completely anonymous.

VR Meeting

VR (Virtual Reality) meeting feature simulates a real space where patients can meet, talk, and interact with mental health professionals. This feature is essential since it provides students with a virtual space where they can talk about their illnesses without having to go to the UMD health center. One of the main reasons students avoided mental health treatment was lack of time (Czyz et al., 2013). College students, especially freshman students, are in an adjustment period, and they are more likely to be overwhelmed with assignments. In addition, trying to fit in additional commitment to seek treatment causes even more stress for students. Thus, our feature provides a virtual space where students can interact with mental health professionals for various treatments. Students will be able to save significant time by excluding traveling time. In addition, it will be economically beneficial because students will be able to save travel expenses.

Appointment Scheduler/Meet Outside

The appointment scheduler feature will provide students with an opportunity to set up appointments without having to go to the health center. With this feature, students will be able to set up appointments easily by logging in to our application. When students create their accounts, our system will ask for their personal information and UID; thus, students do not have to type in additional information when scheduling appointments. Students will be able to easily schedule their appointment because our system shows a calendar with available dates and times. Another feature called Meet Outside provides students with an opportunity to seek mental health professionals outside of the normal healthcare environment. It will provide students comfort by selecting an environment where students are familiar to interact with professionals. Furthermore, walking meetings can boost mental and physical health by improving “self-perception and self-esteem, mood and sleep quality, and it reduces stress and anxiety” (Walkingforhealth, n.d.).

24 Hour Hotline

Another important feature that our system provides is a 24-hour hotline. It is free and confidential support for students in distress and experiencing mental issues. It can be a good starting point where students can obtain information, resources, and support with mental health illnesses. With trained professionals’ help, students will be able to reduce the mental illness that they are suffering at the moment. Also, since it is confidential and anonymous, students won’t have any pressure talking about the current issue that they are experiencing. Furthermore, it will encourage students to seek mental health treatment for those who have a preference for self-management.

  4.
