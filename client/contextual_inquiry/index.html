Contextual Inquiry

Describe

Regarding preparation for the interviews, our team decided that we wanted to generate questions that were focused on the freshman year experience. We came up with these questions by cross-referencing them with Salma’s tips on interviews. This was to make sure our questions were letting the interviewee explain and not limit them to binary or short responses. We practiced by answering the questions ourselves, seeing if we were able to provide thoughtful responses. We wanted to give our interviewees the chance to express themselves fully about what they felt during their first year of college. This allows us to take a lot more into consideration moving forward into the next phases of the UX design process.

Interview Questions

  • Reflecting back on your freshman year, what was the general state of your mental health at the beginning of the year?
  • What was the general state of your mental health at the end of the year?
  • What factors would you say contributed to you feeling that way?
  • Did you ever seek help over your mental health freshman year from an on-campus resource? If so, how was the experience seeking that resource?
  • Did you ever seek help over your mental health freshman year from an off-campus resource? If so, how was the experience seeking that resource?
  • In what ways would you say those resources helped you? In what ways would you say those resources did not help you?
  • How was your experience with the UMD health center and its resources? Would you say that they have the proper resources to help freshmen with mental health issues?
  • From your experience, what would you like to see UMD do in order to better serve incoming freshmen with mental health issues?

Report

Since our client is the UMD health center, we’ve focused our observation around the UMD health center building found on campus. Collectively, our group plans to conduct multiple interviews in order to gain a better understanding of the mental health problem at UMD. For this report, however, we will focus on one interview in particular with Joe. Joe is a senior Information Science major. Joe opened up to us about the state of his mental health during his freshman year. We’ve created categories of waiting, navigation, general mood, type of traveler, behavior, and age for our team's observational guide. We focused on how long people waited around the health center to get treated. Then we observed how people navigated around the building, the general mood of the people utilizing the health center, coming alone or in pairs, behavior around the health center, and the overall age group of people using the health center.

Subset of Data

Observation notes

When you approach the health center, one thing that stands out is that there is a large screening board right in the road to the entrance, as if the health center wants visitors to look at the screening before entering. I stopped in front of the health facility and read the screening board because I hadn't been there since freshman year. From this little experience, I concluded there is a high chance that someone who hasn’t been to the health center in a while would stop and read the screening board. From my observations, for 2 hours most of the people who visited seemed to be aware of the screening board because they went into the health center without even giving the screening board a glance when entering. There were a few people, like me, who checked the screening board. Given how long they spent reading the screening board, I believe they were a first-time visitor. During my two hours, only once did a couple of students enter the health center with bags and exit a few minutes later without them, implying that they were simply there to drop the bag. We also had a couple of kids who ignored the screening board as if they just saw the sign as irrelevant and went into the health center but seconds later came out to read the screening board. There have also been instances where students ignored the screening board and instead sat on the benches in front of the health center, checking their phones or watches to see before going in, suggesting that they might have made an appointment beforehand. Overall from this observation, I would say that the majority of traffic suggested that they knew about the screening, suggesting that they somehow knew about the health center and their current restrictions regarding getting appointments or visitations.

Reflections

Overall, our contextual inquiry went as we planned. We conducted interviews and observed UMD Health Center as we planned. The division of work among our team members allowed each member to focus on one task. Through our observation, we got to see how people behaved and utilized the health center. We were able to understand their needs and overall tasks. Through our interviews, we were able to obtain the mental state of students during freshman year and how they dealt with those issues. Although many more tasks are to be completed, our team feels like we have a big picture understanding of designing ideas for supporting UMD freshmen. If we had time to do more interviews and observations, our team would focus our interviews on freshmen students. It would be helpful for us to understand their current mental state and if freshmen students are aware of support for mental health disorders. For the observation site, we would observe where freshmen are commonly seen to observe the overall mood of freshmen students to see if they are stressed or anxious.
