Concept Statement

The UMD Mental Health Assistance System will replace the old behavioral health services provided by our client: the University of Maryland Health Center. It will provide 24- hour-a-day services to general students, especially to support freshmen students seeking mental health assistance. This service includes online mental health assistance for those who want to have an anonymous setting, for those seeking other resources, and for those who would like to meet outside of the UMD Health Center. The new system includes anonymous chatting, appointment scheduler, opportunity to meet outside of the health center, and VR meetings for those who live away from the campus and canâ€™t commute to get assistance. The new design will be more inclusive of disabled students who wish to seek mental health treatment; our design will include various features, for example, a video with clear audio and subtitles to discuss treatment methods and processes to provide clear guidance. Furthermore, to acknowledge students of our existence, we will send weekly emails to students to seek our services if they are suffering from mental health disorders. Overall, previous behavioral health services failed to provide a variety of options, resulting in extensive waiting time and avoidance in seeking treatment. Compared to behavioral health services, our system will provide a variety of options to encourage students to seek mental health assistance comfortably. We will implement a UX solution that guides and encourages our users, primarily freshmen students, who experience mental health issues to seek our system

Implemented Takeaways From User Testing

The change that weâ€™ve made to our new design is that our team included upcoming appointments on the home page. This way, without having to go to the appointment page, users will be able to see their appointment status from the home page. This design change will remind users of their approved appointment time and date; it will be much easier to track down their appointment schedule. Weâ€™ve implemented this design idea because one of our participants mentioned that it will be a good idea to add the appointment status on our home page so users can check their appointment status more easily. This was a takeaway from our most recent user testing, where the user thought the original design had too many words and the format was cluttered, so we simplified it with our current design.

On the first screen of the App, we've added an extra choice from our original design. By placing them in the Menu area, we've made it clearer what these various services are. Another addition is the Home section, which, as previously said, allows users to keep track of their appointment schedule and reminds them of their scheduled time and date. This takeaway comes from our research into other apps that include a menu option. Another reason we added a Menu area was because our users wanted a means to check the progress of their appointments, and this option would be useful.

Although not much has changed from the previous look, the user now has the option of changing the font size. We reasoned that users with any type of vision impairment could benefit from the font size modification option. If a user is using the anonymous chat late at night without their contacts or glasses, they no longer need to be glued to the screen to see the messages and can instead use the new option. Following user testing, we can add a read at (time) indication to the messages so that the user can see that the anonymous messages they are sending are being read and that they can assist.

Essentially the same as our previous design, with the exception that we now have clear sentences for our users, such as how to get started watching the instructional video and a note at the bottom informing them that the link they click will take them somewhere else, to give them a heads-up on what will happen if they click the link. This was a takeaway from one of our thinking aloud evaluations from Part 1, in which the user suggested that having a small note indicating what the link would do would be helpful.

We made a significant improvement to our Schedule Appointment menu in that our users can now schedule their date and time at the same time. Previously, the user had to select data from the calendar first, then go to the time area to choose the time. Now with the new design, users can select a date and then, at the bottom of the calendar, a time option that allows users to slide to any time that is convenient for the appointment. This was a takeaway from another user test, where the user thought it would be easier to alter the date and time from the same location rather than having to choose one after the other.

From our previous design, when users set the date and time for the mental health treatment, there werenâ€™t any confirmation messages that let users know if their appointment was accepted or not. Without this design change, users may submit multiple appointments because they wonâ€™t have any idea whether their appointment was accepted or not. With the new design idea, when users confirm their appointment, a confirmation page will pop up to let users know that their appointment has been successfully scheduled. This was a takeaway from user testing; the user thought it would be nice to have a confirmation page to let users know their appointments have been successfully scheduled.

Our previous design didnâ€™t provide users with enough detail about the Meet Outside feature; it only provided date, time, and location. Although the name of the feature can be self-explanatory, users may not know about this feature and disregard it. Now with the new design, we provided an explanatory statement about the Meet Outside feature to provide enough detail to users who are not familiar with this feature. Now for those who want to try a new treatment feature, users know how to set up an appointment to have a chance to meet with mental health assistance outside of UMD health center. This was a takeaway from one of our thinking aloud evaluations from Part 1. The user recommended having a leading page; however, we considered having an explanatory statement because it was more concise and straightforward to provide details about this feature.
