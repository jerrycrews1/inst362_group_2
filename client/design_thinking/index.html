Design Thinking

Concept Statement

The UMD Mental Health Assistance System will replace the old behavioral health services provided by our client: the University of Maryland Health Center. It will provide 24-hour-a-day services to general students, especially to support freshmen students suffering from mental health disorders. This service includes online mental health assistance for those who want to have an anonymous setting. The new system includes chatting and video calls for those who live away from the campus. Setting appointments with mental health assistants will also be available through our system for those who want to have personal interaction. Furthermore, to acknowledge students of our existence, we will send weekly emails to students to seek our services if they are suffering from mental health disorders. Overall, previous behavioral health services failed to provide a variety of options, resulting in extensive waiting time and avoidance in seeking treatment. Compared to behavioral health services, our system will provide a variety of options to encourage students to seek mental health assistance comfortably. We will implement a UX solution that guides and encourages our users, freshmen students, who experience mental health issues to seek our system.

Personas

Our team split up the task of creating personas. Once each individual created their respective persona, the other members of the group checked over them. The personas came from the groups of users we identified while during our Work Activity Affinity Diagram.

Persona: Arham Ali

Arham Ali loves engineering and is fascinated by aerospace so hence when he entered university of Maryland he decided to pursue his degree in aerospace engineering. He loves playing sports and going to the gym. Unfortunately for him he got injured playing sports and cannot walk long distances and needs multiple teams if he is walking. He is still able to work out and go to work so in his free time he tries to occupy himself by taking care of his body by going to the gym.

He loves making jokes in a social setting. Always trying to create the best atmosphere and making sure that everyone is happy. His strong personality in a social setting sometimes backfires on him as it is hard for him to talk about his problems and stressful issues. He is active on most of his social networks.

Key characteristics include that Arham is Age 19-22. Is very sociable and loves making jokes and making sure that his friends are safe. Thinks of himself as the oldest sibling of his friend group. His goals include making sure that he is in very good condition physically and emotionally. Making sure that he graduates on time.

Scenarios

The same individuals who created the personas were responsible for creating the corresponding scenario.

Scenario: Arham Ali

Arham Ali is a senior aeronautical engineering student who transferred from another university to the University of Maryland College Park. The majority of his classes are on the north east side of campus, which is home to all of the engineering facilities. He lives in one of the flats near college and goes to the gym during his free time and when he is stressed. When he's weary and stressed, he usually doesn't feel like going uphill to the stamp or the health center due to his long term injury and also because of how far it is from his classes and the inaccessibility of buses to take him to stamp. He would want to see an online mental health session from the health center during such days when he can’t make these trips to the health center. He also finds it inconvenient that the health center does not have information on where else he can go on campus if he needed to talk about his stress.

Ideation

This part of the project was really fun. We started by brainstorming design ideas. We did this without any critique and without any reservations.

Some of ideas were:

  • Freshman receive a class on mental health resources during orientation.
  • Students can use VR headsets to get mental health assistance.
  • Popup mental health centers around campus with QR code to scan for help.

Sketches

We chose 10 of these ideas to turn into sketches. Some of the sketches involved two or three ideas because they were similar.
